‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs - Prepare for Hazards

Warning signs give you advance notice of potential dangers. When you see one, reduce speed, increase awareness, and be ready to respond.

üö∏ Pedestrian Warnings

  • School crossing patrols
  • Pedestrian crossing zones
  • Children playing areas
Slow down near schools and residential areas

üåßÔ∏è Road Condition Warnings

  • Slippery when wet or icy
  • Uneven road surface
  • Loose chippings
Adjust speed for poor conditions

üîÑ Road Layout Warnings

  • Sharp bends and curves
  • Roundabouts approaching
  • Road narrows ahead
Check speed before reaching hazard

üöß Temporary Warnings

  • Road works in progress
  • Temporary traffic signals
  • Loose stones from construction
Follow temporary speed limits
üí° Memory Trick: "Triangle = Trouble might be ahead" - Always means potential danger or hazard

üî¥ Regulatory Signs - Know the Rules

These signs show traffic laws you must obey. Ignoring them can result in fines, penalty points, or test failure.

üö´ Red Circle = PROHIBITION

What you CANNOT do

  • Speed Limits: Maximum speed allowed (30, 40, 50, 60, 70 mph)
  • No Entry: Absolutely forbidden to enter
  • Stop Signs: Must come to complete stop
  • Give Way: Yield to other traffic
  • No Parking: Stopping prohibited at specific times
  • No Overtaking: Cannot pass other vehicles

üîµ Blue Circle = MANDATORY

What you MUST do

  • Turn Directions: Must turn in indicated direction
  • Keep Left/Right: Must stay on specified side
  • Roundabout: Must circulate as directed
  • Minimum Speed: Must maintain at least this speed
  • Shared Use Paths: Designated for cyclists/pedestrians
üí° Memory Trick: "Red STOPS you, Blue ORDERS you" - Red says "don't", Blue says "must"

üõ£Ô∏è Road Markings - Lines That Matter

Markings painted on the road are just as important as upright signs. They provide information and regulate traffic.

White Line Rules

  • Broken white line: Marks lanes, cross when safe
  • Single solid white line: Don't cross except for access or avoiding stationary obstacle
  • Double white (solid on your side): MUST NOT cross
  • Double white (broken on your side): May cross if safe to overtake

Special Markings

  • Yellow lines: Parking/waiting restrictions (check nearby signs for times)
  • Zig-zag lines: No stopping or parking (school zones, crossings)
  • Box junction: Yellow criss-cross - don't enter unless exit is clear
  • STOP/SLOW text: Instructions painted at junctions
üí° Memory Trick: "More paint = More important" - Solid lines are more restrictive than broken

üìñ Smart Study Strategies

1Ô∏è‚É£ Learn by Shape First, Then Details

Start with understanding what each shape means (triangle=warning, circle=order, rectangle=info). This gives you immediate context before you even look at the symbol inside.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Group Similar Signs Together

Study related signs as a family: all speed limits together, all pedestrian warnings together. This helps you notice the differences between similar-looking signs.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Use Real-World Practice

When traveling as a passenger, identify every sign you see. Explain what it means and what action a driver should take. Real roads = real learning!

4Ô∏è‚É£ Test Yourself Regularly

Don't just read about signs - actively recall them. Use our Road and Traffic Signs quiz (67 questions with 85 images) to test your knowledge repeatedly.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Understand the WHY

Don't just memorize - understand why signs are placed where they are. A "children crossing" sign near a school makes sense. Context helps memory.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Create Mental Stories

Link signs to memorable stories or images. "The red circle is an angry face saying NO!" Silly associations stick in memory better than facts.

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid These Common Mistakes

‚ùå Confusing Similar Signs

Many signs look alike but have different meanings. Pay attention to small details inside the shape.

‚úÖ Fix: Study signs side-by-side to spot differences

‚ùå Ignoring Sign Colors

Colors aren't decorative - they're meaningful. Blue, green, and white backgrounds tell you road types.

‚úÖ Fix: Learn color coding system first

‚ùå Forgetting Road Markings

Students often focus on upright signs and forget painted markings are equally tested.

‚úÖ Fix: Dedicate time specifically to road markings

‚ùå Not Practicing Enough

Reading about signs once isn't enough - you need repeated exposure and active recall.

‚úÖ Fix: Use spaced repetition: study today, again in 3 days, again in a week
